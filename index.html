Abstract

Thermal multi-object tracking (MOT) is essential for surveillance and safety-critical perception in low-visibility environments, yet it remains challenging due to weak appearance cues, low contrast, frequent occlusions, and unstable object boundaries. We present a three-stage framework for thermal pedestrian MOT on the TP-MOT benchmark, consisting of thermal pedestrian detection, online tracking, and offline trajectory refinement. Our main contribution is a lightweight post-processing module that treats identity continuity as a first-class objective by stitching fragmented tracklets using temporal continuity, spatial proximity, motion consistency, and border-aware constraints. Unlike approaches that rely on heavy re-identification models or costly global optimization, the proposed refinement stage improves identity preservation while allowing the online tracker to remain simple and efficient. Experiments on the PBVS Thermal MOT benchmark show that the proposed refinement stage consistently reduces fragmentation and improves continuity over raw tracking outputs, yielding a robust and practical real-time solution for thermal pedestrian surveillance.

Challenges & Our Approach

 Thermal MOT Challenges

  • No clothing texture — only heat signatures
  • Low contrast between pedestrians and background
  • Frequent occlusion and overlapping targets
  • Non-linear motion & abrupt scale changes
  • Heavy Re-ID models increase cost prohibitively

 Our Key Insight

  • Stage-wise parameter tuning over complex models
  • Lightweight SORT-based online tracker
  • Online short-gap identity repair (Δt ≤ 7, d ≤ 60 px)
  • Offline motion-consistent tracklet stitching
  • No Re-ID needed — 96,000× less memory than SAM3

Offline Motion-Consistent Tracklet Stitching

For an old tracklet a and a new tracklet b, we extrapolate the endpoint of a using a 3-point velocity estimate and merge b into a only when all of the following criteria are satisfied:

c̃a = cenda + va · Δt  where  Δt = tstartb − tenda
1
Border-Aware

Both endpoints lie outside a 60-pixel border band (no border entry/exit)

2
Temporal Gap

1 ≤ Δt ≤ 30 frames

3
Spatial Proximity

‖c̃a − cstartb‖₂ ≤ 80 pixels

4
Motion Consistency

Angle ≤ 45° and speed ratio ≤ 3.0 (if speed ≥ 0.25 px/frame)
